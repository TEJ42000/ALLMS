## Google Chrome

### **Method 1: Private Window (Easiest - Recommended)**

This is the simplest way and works every time!

#### **Step 1: Close All Browser Windows**
- Look at the top of your screen
- Click the **X** on every Chrome window to close them all
- Make sure Chrome is completely closed

#### **Step 2: Open a Private Window**

**On Windows:**
1. Right-click the Chrome icon on your desktop or taskbar
2. Click **"New Incognito Window"**

**On Mac:**
1. Click the Chrome icon in your dock
2. At the top menu, click **"File"**
3. Click **"New Incognito Window"**

**Keyboard Shortcut (Faster):**
- Windows: Press `Ctrl` + `Shift` + `N` at the same time
- Mac: Press `Cmd` + `Shift` + `N` at the same time

#### **Step 3: You'll See a Dark Window**
- The window will be dark/black colored
- You'll see a spy/detective icon
- This means you're in private mode ‚úÖ

#### **Step 4: Go to the Website**
1. Click in the address bar at the top
2. Type (or copy and paste): `https://lls-study-portal-sarfwmfd3q-ez.a.run.app`
3. Press **Enter**

#### **Step 5: Log In**
1. You'll see the LLS Study Portal page
2. Click the **"Login with Google"** button
3. Choose your account: **amberunal13@gmail.com**
4. Enter your password if asked
5. **You should now be logged in!** ‚úÖ

## ‚ùì **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Q: Why do I need to do this?**
A: Your browser remembered that you were previously denied access. We need to clear that memory so it checks again and sees that you now have access.

### **Q: Will I need to do this every time?**
A: No! Only this first time. After you log in successfully once, you can use the normal browser window from then on.

### **Q: What if it still doesn't work?**
A: Contact the administrator (matej@mgms.eu) and let them know. They can help troubleshoot further.

### **Q: Is the private/incognito window safe?**
A: Yes! It's completely safe. It just means your browser won't remember your history or cookies for that session.

### **Q: Can I use my phone or tablet?**
A: Yes! The same steps work on mobile browsers. Look for "New Incognito Tab" or "New Private Tab" in your mobile browser's menu.
