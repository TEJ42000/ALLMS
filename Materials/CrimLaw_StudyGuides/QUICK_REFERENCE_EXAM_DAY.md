### Self-Defense (7 Criteria - ALL Must Be Met)

1. ‚úì Defense of **legitimate interests** (life, body, property, others)
2. ‚úì Attack is **imminent** (happening now or about to happen)
3. ‚úì Attack is **unlawful**
4. ‚úì Defense is **necessary** (no reasonable alternative)
5. ‚úì Defense is **proportionate** (reasonable force)
6. ‚úì **Subjective element** (belief attack is occurring)
7. ‚úì **No prior fault** (didn't provoke it)

### Causation (2 Steps)

**Step 1: Factual Causation (But-For Test)**  
"But for D's act, would result have occurred when and as it did?"
- NO ‚Üí Proceed to Step 2
- YES ‚Üí No liability

**Step 2: Legal Causation**  
Can result be legally attributed despite intervening cause?
- Foreseeable natural event ‚Üí Usually YES
- High intent (dolus) ‚Üí Consequences "never too remote"
- Highly culpable third party ‚Üí Usually NO (breaks chain)
- Unforeseeable victim conduct ‚Üí Usually NO
- Victim predisposition ‚Üí Usually YES (thin skull rule)

### Mens Rea Hierarchy (Highest to Lowest)

1. **Direct Intent** - Purpose/aim to cause result
2. **Indirect Intent** - Necessary consequence of purposeful act
3. **Dolus Eventualis** - Consciously accepting risk (NL/DE: intent; other systems: recklessness)
4. **Conscious Negligence** - Aware of risk but think can avoid
5. **Unconscious Negligence** - Should have been aware of risk

### Engel Criteria (Is it "Criminal"?)

### Article 6 ECHR Fair Trial Rights

**6(1)**: Fair + public hearing by independent, impartial tribunal  
**6(2)**: Presumption of innocence  
**6(3)**: Minimum defense rights

**Key Principles:**
- Applies to **pre-trial** proceedings (Salduz)
- **Overall fairness** assessment
- Privilege against **self-incrimination**
- Right to **remain silent**
- Right to **lawyer from first interrogation**

### Salduz Test (Right to Lawyer)

**Rule**: Access to lawyer required from **first interrogation**

**Exceptions?** Only if:
- Compelling reasons exist, AND
- Did not irreparably prejudice defense rights, AND
- Overall proceedings remained fair

**Statements without lawyer?**
- Generally inadmissible
- Assess: Could defendant challenge later?
- Consider: Overall fairness of proceedings

### Jalloh/G√§fgen (Evidence from Art. 3 Violation)

**Art. 3 Categories** (ascending severity):
1. Degrading treatment
2. Inhuman treatment  
3. Torture

**Evidence Rules:**
- **Torture** ‚Üí ALWAYS inadmissible (automatic Art. 6 violation)
- **Inhuman/degrading** ‚Üí Inadmissible if decisive for conviction
- Assess: Overall fairness of proceedings

**Jalloh**: Forced emetics for drugs = degrading + unfair trial  
**G√§fgen**: Threat of pain = torture BUT no Art. 6 violation (conviction based on untainted confession)

### Schatschaschwili Test (Absent Witnesses)

**Three-Step Interrelated Test for Art. 6(3)(d):**

#### Step 1: Good Reason for Non-Attendance?
- Death, fear, untraceable?
- Did state make reasonable efforts?

#### Step 2: Sole or Decisive Evidence?
- Could convict without it?
- Not rigid exclusion rule
- Greater decisiveness ‚Üí stronger counterbalancing needed

#### Step 3: Counterbalancing Factors?
- Could defendant challenge reliability?
- Opportunity at pre-trial stage?
- Procedural safeguards?
- Corroboration?
- Judicial scrutiny?

**CRITICAL**: Steps are **interrelated** - assess overall fairness holistically!
