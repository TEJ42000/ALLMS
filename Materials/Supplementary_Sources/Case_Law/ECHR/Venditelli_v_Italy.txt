## **ğŸ¯ THE TWO-STEP TEST** *(Memorize this!)*

### **STEP 1: Establish the Relevant Period**

**Start Date:** When the **criminal charge** begins

- Official notification of allegation
- OR when situation substantially affected by investigation

**End Date:** When the decision becomes **final**

- Not just when judgment is delivered
- When all appeals exhausted or time for appeal expires

**Calculate:** Total duration of proceedings

-----

### **STEP 2: Assess Reasonableness Using 4 Factors**

#### **Factor 1: Complexity of the Case**

**Ask:**

- Was it factually complex?
- Were there many witnesses/parties?
- Was the legal issue complicated?
- Were there technical issues to resolve?

**Principle:** More complex â†’ longer proceedings acceptable

-----

#### **Factor 2: Conduct of the Applicant**

**Ask:**

- Did the applicant cause delays?
- Were postponement requests legitimate?
- Did applicant use delaying tactics?

**Important:** Legitimate use of procedural rights is acceptable, but abuse is not

**Examples of conduct that causes delay:**

- Requesting postponements (even if legitimate)
- Filing numerous motions
- Changing lawyers
- Being unavailable for hearings

-----

#### **Factor 3: Conduct of the Authorities**

**Ask:**

- Did state authorities cause unnecessary delays?
- Were there administrative inefficiencies?
- Did courts postpone without good reason?
- Were there periods of inactivity by prosecution/court?

**Principle:** State is responsible for organizing efficient judicial system

**Critical:** Delays caused by authorities weigh **heavily against the state**

-----

#### **Factor 4: What is at Stake for the Applicant?** *(Not discussed in Ventelli but from previous case law)*

**Ask:**

- How severe is the potential penalty?
- What impact on applicantâ€™s life?
- Is liberty at stake?

**Principle:** Higher stakes â†’ greater urgency required â†’ shorter proceedings expected
