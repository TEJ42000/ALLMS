üí° Fundamental Principles

Central Paradox

Enlightenment Ideal: Judges as "mouths of the law" (bouches de la loi) - mechanical application

Reality: ALL texts require interpretation. Judges MUST interpret to apply abstract rules to specific facts.

Formal vs Material

Formal: Focus on HOW law is made (proper enactment)

Material: Focus on WHAT law says (must be just)

This distinction appears throughout all legal systems!

Legality Principle

Latin: Nullum crimen, nulla poena sine lege

Meaning: No crime, no punishment without law

Source: Cesare Beccaria (1764)

Radbruch Formula

Statement: "Extremely unjust law is not law at all"

Application: Nazi-era laws, Wall Shooters Case

Principle: Material justice can override formal legality

üéØ Exam Preparation Tips

Essay Structure:

  1. Introduction - State the legal question clearly
  2. Legal Framework - Explain applicable rules and principles
  3. Application - Apply rules to specific facts of the case
  4. Reasoning - Show which interpretation method you're using
  5. Conclusion - Provide clear answer to the question

What to Focus On:

  • HOW judges interpret rules (not just memorizing statutes)
  • The central paradox (Enlightenment ideal vs. reality)
  • Facts, question, reasoning, and decision for each major case
  • Connections across weeks (formal/material distinction everywhere)
  • Timeline showing Roman law's influence on modern codification
