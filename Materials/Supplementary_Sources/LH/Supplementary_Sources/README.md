## ğŸ§  ESSENTIAL COURSE CONTENT

### Core Mnemonics (Memorize These!)

**C-O-E (Codification Elements):**
- **C** = Committed to writing
- **O** = Official authority
- **E** = Exclusive source

**GHTCS (Interpretation Methods):**
- **G** = Grammatical
- **H** = Historical
- **T** = Teleological
- **C** = Contextual
- **S** = Sociological

**K-R-E (Roman Evolution):**
- **K** = Kingdom
- **R** = Republic
- **E** = Empire

### Must-Know Cases

| Case | Year | Principle |
|------|------|-----------|
| Bear Case | 1990 | Legality principle |
| Nix v. Hedden | 1893 | Common usage interpretation |
| People v. Nelson | 2011 | Purposive interpretation |
| Marbury v. Madison | 1803 | Judicial review |
| Popov v. Hayashi | 2002 | Roman law concepts |
| Nuremberg Trials | 1945-46 | Unjust law not valid |
| Wall Shooters | 1990s | Radbruch Formula |
| Young v. Bristol | 1944 | Stare decisis |
| Bulmer v. Bollinger | 1974 | EU law supremacy |

### Justinian's 4-Part Codification

1. **DIGEST** - 50 books, jurist law, NO lex posterior
2. **INSTITUTES** - Student textbook, official law
3. **CODEX** - Imperial legislation, YES lex posterior
4. **NOVELS** - Post-Codex laws

## ğŸ’¡ KEY PRINCIPLES

**Central Paradox:**
- Enlightenment ideal: Judges as "mouths of the law"
- Reality: ALL texts require interpretation

**Formal vs. Material:**
- Formal = HOW law is made
- Material = WHAT law says (must be just)

**Radbruch Formula:**
- "Extremely unjust law is not law at all"

**Legality Principle:**
- *Nullum crimen, nulla poena sine lege*
- No crime, no punishment without law
