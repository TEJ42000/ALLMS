## â• Adding New Materials

### Official Lecture Materials
1. Place in appropriate subject directory: `Course_Materials/[Subject]/Part_A_Substantive/`
2. Organize by week if applicable: `Week_01/`, `Week_02/`, etc.
3. Use descriptive names: `Week_3_Lecture_Slides.pdf`

### Student Notes
1. Place in `Study_Notes/` subdirectory
2. Include author or source: `Criminal_Law_Notes_Student.pdf`
3. Organize by week or topic

### Working Group Materials
1. Place in `Working_Groups/` subdirectory
2. Include week number: `Week_4_WG_Exercises.pdf`
3. Keep exercises and solutions together

### Study Guides
1. Place in `Study_Guides/` subdirectory
2. Update `FILE_INDEX.md` with new materials
3. Use clear, descriptive names

## ğŸ“ File Naming Convention

**Good Examples:**
- `Week_3_Lecture_Criminal_Law_Part_A.pdf`
- `CL_Part_B_Week_5_Complete_Notes.docx`
- `Working_Group_Week_4_Exercises.pdf`

- Unclear names: `notes.pdf`, `lecture.pptx`
- Special characters: `Week 3 (Part A).pdf`
- Inconsistent formatting: `wk3.pdf`, `Week_04.pdf`
